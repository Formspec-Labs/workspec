## §8.4 — Eligibility brackets (illustrative simplified)

| Household size | Monthly gross income limit |
|---|---|
| 1 | $1,473 |
| 2 | $1,984 |
| 3 | $2,495 |
| 4+ | $2,495 + $511 per additional person |

These limits are derived from federal poverty thresholds. Households exceeding the gross-income limit are categorically ineligible UNLESS exempted under §273.10(d).

## §8.5 — Redetermination interval (state-specific)

Texas adopts the federal §273.10(a) interval framework with one state-specific addition:

- **Households with seasonal agricultural income** are redetermined every **3 months** during the active growing season (Apr–Sep) and every 6 months otherwise. This is narrower than federal §273.10(a)(1)'s 6-month default.

## §8.7 — Spanish-language notices (state-mandated)

Per State Code §531.0227, Texas SNAP notices MUST be issued in Spanish for all households where Spanish is the primary spoken language as recorded in the case file. The Spanish notice MUST be substantively equivalent to the English notice; any divergence is grounds for re-issue.

This requirement is **state-narrower-than-federal**: federal §273.10(g)(3) requires Spanish at minimum; Texas additionally requires substantive-equivalence verification.
